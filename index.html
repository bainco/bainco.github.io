Welcome to my Searle Fellows ePortfolio!

Right now, this page’s content is tailored towards the Searle Fellows program (offered by the Searle Center for Advancing Teaching and Learning at Northwestern university). However, in the future I plan to expand it with more teaching related content. Think of it as a living portfolio of teaching ideas that are running around in my head at any given time.

I’m Connor (Dr. Bain) – I’m an Assistant Professor of Instruction in the Computer Science Department at Northwestern University. While my focus is primarily teaching, I’m also a researcher at the intersection of Learning Sciences and Computer science, specializing in ideas of computational literacy and CS teacher pedagogy in the K-12 space. As a professor at Northwestern, I mainly teach in the CS Intro sequence and manage some of the largest courses at Northwestern. My core offerings right now are:

  • COMP_SCI 111 - Fundamentals of Computer Programming I – (~500 students a quarter)
  • COMP_SCI 110 - Intro to Computer Programming – (~250 students a quarter)
  • COMP_SCI 396 - Communicating Computer Science (~40 students a quarter)

I’m also in the process of developing a couple of new classes for the 2024-2025 school year including a new COMP_SCI 397 course focused on Short-Course / Workshop design for senior CS majors interested in CS pedagogy and course design.

As part of the Searle Fellows Program, I was mainly focused on augmenting my COMP_SCI 110 course. This is a challenging course to teach because it’s:

  • the only course in the entire Computer Science Department which is majority non-major students
  • the only course CS course that specifically does not count toward a major or minor in CS
  • a large-format course (250 to 350 students per quarter)
  • has a majority of students who simply want to see what CS is rather than become expert programmers (~60% according to surveys)

Specifically for the Searle Fellows Project, I made a number of changes and new assignments:

  • instituted a new pre-course survey to get a better idea of the incoming students
  • integrated a new core learning objective: Students will be able to understand and critique a program written by another person.
  • created a series of new assignments focused on reading programs rather than writing them
  • developed automated assignment and grading tools for peer reviews of computer programs via the Canvas LMS
  • begun a prototype of a new piece of software that integrates with Canvas capable of supporting peer reviewing of programming assignments

While we were unable to formally evaluate the specific changes made to the syllabus and course, I’ll do my best to include anecdotes and informal qualitative evidence of the changes this project may have caused in the course.

In addition to the project elements (found on the Projects page), you’ll find a series of book theme reflections from the various Searle Fellows Salons as well as an “evolving” teaching statement that has been updated as I’ve progressed through the program.

Thanks to the Searle Fellows Program and a special thanks to my Mentor, Dr. Michael Fagen and Project Group Leader Kate Flom Derrick.
