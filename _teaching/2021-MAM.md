This course will covers various topics related to the discipline of computer simulation through multi-agent modeling (also called agent-based modeling). Multi-agent modeling specifically focuses on the study of systems where a large number of independent entities (agents) simultaneously interact with each other, resulting in emergent patterns of behavior for the system as a whole.

In particular, students will gain proficiency with NetLogo, which is a sophisticated (yet easy to learn) language and environment for multi-agent modeling. Previous computer programming experience will prove helpful, but is not required.

Teaching Assistants play a key role in the course, namely sheparding students to complete a 5-week project during which they create a publication-quality agent-based model. <a href="http://ccl.northwestern.edu/courses/mam2019/student_work.shtml">You can take a look at student projects by visiting clicking here.</a>
